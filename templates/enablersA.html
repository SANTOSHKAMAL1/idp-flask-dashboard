Governance Framework for IDP Implementation

The Governance Enabler serves as the cornerstone of all enablers in the IDP document, focusing on Centralized Leadership, Policy Management, Strategic Oversight, and Digital Growth.

NEP 2020 UGC Compliance NAAC Standards Digital Transformation

Key Focus Areas

Transparency & Accountability

Ensuring open decision-making through grievance redressal, compliance tracking, and stakeholder engagement.

Stakeholder Engagement

Involving faculty, students, alumni, and external partners in participatory governance.

Digital Transformation

Enhancing administrative efficiency using ERP systems, AI-based decision support, and paperless workflows.

Policy & Framework

Establishing regulatory guidelines and governance structures for effective decision-making.

Compliance & Risk

Maintaining quality assurance, accreditation readiness, and institutional risk assessment.

Sustainability

Developing self-sustaining governance models and integrating ESG practices.

Conclusion

Governance is not simply an administrative function, but the pillar of institutional quality. It guides academic policies, research funding, digital transformation, financial sustainability, and participatory decision-making. The IDP offers a systematic path for governance excellence through short-term, mid-term and long-term reforms.

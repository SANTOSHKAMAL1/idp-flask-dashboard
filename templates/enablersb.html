Financial Enablers Framework

Sustainable Resource Generation for Institutional Excellence

JAIN (Deemed to be University) is committed to maintain a high growth trajectory through a planned approach towards sourcing of funds needed for capital expenditure and recurring expenditure, deploying funds optimally and generating a surplus, which can be redeployed to meet future needs and to avail opportunities that are bound to arise in India's vibrant economy.

Current Funding Diversification 45%

Strategic Financial Objectives

Our financial strategy focuses on three key pillars: Diversification of revenue sources, Optimization of resource allocation, and Sustainability through endowment growth and strategic investments. By 2025, we aim to reduce tuition fee dependence to under 35% while increasing research and industry partnerships to 40% of total revenue.

Strategic initiatives to reduce dependency on tuition fees and build sustainable revenue streams

  • Avail Government funded schemes through spreading awareness and supporting efforts of faculty members/research scholars to secure funds.

Revenue Growth Strategy

The university aims to gradually reduce dependence on tuition fees by developing multiple revenue streams including research grants, industry partnerships, technology commercialization, and alumni contributions. Our target is to achieve a 40-30-30 revenue mix (Tuition-Research-Other) by 2025.

2.6 Financial / Investment Committee

The Financial Committee comprises senior administrators, finance experts, and external financial professionals to ensure diverse perspectives in financial decision-making. The committee meets quarterly to review financial performance and strategic investments.
